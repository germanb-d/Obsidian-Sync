# Welcome to Syro: your incremental reading flow starts here

Hi! This is the first tutorial note that Syro created for you automatically, and it is already tagged with `#tutorial`. If you open the **Syro review queue sidebar** now, you will see this note sitting inside the `NEW NOTES`
 section.

## 1. Meet the Review Queue

When a long article or dense paper feels too large to finish in one sitting, Syro helps you break it into smaller reading sessions.

- **Grouped queues**: every note you track appears in the right sidebar and is grouped into sections such as `New`, `Today`, and `Overdue`.
- **Tags keep you focused**: this tutorial note already has the `#tutorial` tag. Type `#tutorial` into the sidebar filter to instantly narrow the queue to just tutorial material.

## 2. Try the Timeline

This is the core of Syro's incremental reading workflow.

1. **Open the Timeline panel**: below this note in the sidebar you will find a `TIMELINE: Syro Sidebar Review Tutorial` panel. Expand it if it is collapsed.
2. **Record your current stopping point**:
   - Place your cursor anywhere in this note.
   - In the Timeline input box, write a short checkpoint such as "Learned the basics of the sidebar workflow".
   - Click **`Commit (Ctrl+Enter)`**.
3. **Jump back into context later**: the next time this note returns to your queue, clicking that timeline record will automatically scroll the editor back to the saved position.

## 3. Adjust the schedule when needed

After you review a tracked file, Syro's weighted scheduler picks the next review time automatically. You can still override it whenever you want.

- If the suggested timing does not match your plan, add a duration prefix such as `7d::` to your timeline entry.
- For example, `7d::Continue this section next week` tells Syro to make the note due again in **7 days**.
