## 1) PRESENT SIMPLE

### Uso
Se usa para: hábitos, rutinas, verdades generales, hechos permanentes, instrucciones o horarios.
### Forma afirmativa
**Sujeto + verbo base**
- I work. / You study. / We play.
### Tercera persona singular
Con **he / she / it**, el verbo suele llevar **-s** o **-es**:
- He works. / She watches. / It goes.
### Reglas ortográficas
- watch → watches / go → goes / study → studies
### Forma negativa
**Sujeto + do/does + not + verbo base**
- I do not work. / He does not work. / They do not study.
- Contracciones: **don't** / **doesn't**
### Forma interrogativa
**Do/Does + sujeto + verbo base?**
- Do you work? / Does she study?
### Pregunta negativa
**Don't / Doesn't + sujeto + verbo base?**
- Don't you work? / Doesn't he like coffee?
Se usa cuando hay sorpresa, expectativa o confirmación.
### Señales típicas
always · usually · often · sometimes · never · every day · on Mondays

## 2) PRESENT CONTINUOUS

### Uso
Se usa para: acciones que están ocurriendo ahora, acciones temporales, situaciones cambiantes, planes cercanos en algunos contextos.
### Forma afirmativa
**Sujeto + am/is/are + verbo-ing**
- I am studying. / She is working. / They are playing.
### Forma negativa
**Sujeto + am/is/are + not + verbo-ing**
- I am not studying. / She is not working. / They are not playing.
- Contracciones: **isn't** / **aren't** / **I'm not**
### Forma interrogativa
**Am/Is/Are + sujeto + verbo-ing?**
- Am I studying? / Is she working? / Are they playing?
### Pregunta negativa
**Isn't / Aren't + sujeto + verbo-ing?**
- Isn't she working? / Aren't they coming?
### Reglas de spelling del -ing
make → making · write → writing · run → running · sit → sitting · lie → lying
### Señales típicas
now · at the moment · right now · today · this week · currently

## 3) PAST SIMPLE

### Uso
Se usa para: acciones terminadas en el pasado, secuencias de acciones, hechos del pasado con tiempo definido.
### Forma afirmativa
**Sujeto + past form**
- I worked. / She played. / They went.
### Verbos regulares
Se agrega **-ed**: work → worked · play → played
### Verbos irregulares
Cambian de forma y se memorizan: go → went · buy → bought · have → had · see → saw
### Forma negativa
**Sujeto + did not + verbo base**
- I did not work. / She did not go. / They did not buy.
- Contracción: **didn't**
### Forma interrogativa
**Did + sujeto + verbo base?**
- Did you work? / Did she go? / Did they buy it?
### Pregunta negativa
**Didn't + sujeto + verbo base?**
- Didn't he call you? / Didn't they arrive?
### Error clásico
Después de **did** no va pasado:
- ❌ Did you went? → ✅ **Did you go?**
### Señales típicas
yesterday · last week · last year · ago · in 2020 · the day before

## 4) PRESENT PERFECT

### Uso
Se usa para: experiencias de vida, acciones pasadas con efecto en el presente, acciones recientes, situaciones que empezaron en el pasado y continúan ahora.
### Forma afirmativa
**Sujeto + have/has + past participle**
- I have studied. / She has worked. / They have gone.
### Forma negativa
**Sujeto + have/has not + past participle**
- I have not studied. / She has not worked. / They have not gone.
- Contracciones: **haven't** / **hasn't**
### Forma interrogativa
**Have/Has + sujeto + past participle?**
- Have you studied? / Has she worked? / Have they gone?
### Pregunta negativa
**Haven't / Hasn't + sujeto + past participle?**
- Haven't you seen it? / Hasn't he finished yet?
### Participio pasado
go → gone · see → seen · buy → bought · do → done · have → had
### Marcadores típicos
already · yet · just · ever · never · since · for
### Diferencia importante: Past Simple vs Present Perfect
No los mezcles.
- **Past Simple**: acción terminada en un momento del pasado → I saw that movie yesterday.
- **Present Perfect**: experiencia o vínculo con el presente → I have seen that movie.
Si hay tiempo definido como **yesterday / last year / in 2020**, normalmente va **Past Simple**.
